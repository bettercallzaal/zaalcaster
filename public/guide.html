Getting in

The app is gated by Sign In With Farcaster. Anyone with a Farcaster account can sign in - what you see depends on who you are:

  • The owner (the fid the deploy is configured for) gets everything: posting, inbox, Daily, Grow, Empire, ZOE.
  • Everyone else gets a read-only view: Feed, Channels, Search, and POIDH. No write actions exist anywhere in the guest surface - the server enforces this, not just the UI.

Nothing you do as a guest can post, like, or spend anything on the owner's behalf.

Daily - your morning screen

Open the app, you land here. Top to bottom:

  • GM header - checklist progress for today. Quests tick themselves off when the app sees you actually did the thing (posted a GM, cleared inbox, replied).
  • Launcher grid - one tap to any tab, LinkedIn drafts, My links.
  • Your projects - each brand opens its channel feed + links.
  • Today's checklist - editable, syncs across devices.
  • ZOE - your tracker - see the next section.
  • Scheduled posts - anything queued from the Post tab, with the send time.

ZOE - tasks, decisions, and Ask ZAO

ZOE is the ZAO's assistant agent. It runs elsewhere (Telegram), but zaalcaster plugs into the same two surfaces ZOE works from - so what you do here shows up on ZOE's board, and what ZOE knows is searchable here.

Your tasks (Daily tab)

  • The ZOE card lists your open tasks from the team tracker, soonest due first.
  • Tap done on a task - it completes on the shared board instantly. No Telegram round trip.

Log a decision

  • Type a decision into the input on the ZOE card ("we're going tokenless-first for X") and hit log.
  • It lands in the tracker as a decision record, and ZOE's memory loop mirrors it into the knowledge graph - so it's findable forever.

Ask ZAO (command palette)

  • Hit cmd+K, type a question or topic, pick Ask ZAO.
  • It searches months of meetings and decisions in the knowledge graph and shows the top matches - "what did we decide about the Empire API?" actually gets answered.

Reading: Feed, Inbox, Channels, Search

  • Feed - chips switch between following, for-you, trending, saved bookmarks, mini-app discovery, and any custom list you've built. Tap a cast body for the whole thread; tap a face for the profile.
  • Inbox - everything that needs an answer, priority-sortable. Draft all writes replies in your voice for the whole batch; every send is a two-step confirm. Nothing ever posts without your tap.
  • Channels - your home channels one tap away, plus search and follow for any channel.
  • Search - people and casts, filterable by channel, searches saveable.
  • Deck (header button) - Feed, Inbox, and a channel side by side, columns style.

Posting

  • Compose top-level casts, pick a channel, quote-cast, attach embeds.
  • Schedule - pick a datetime and queue it; a cron sends it even with the app closed.
  • Cross-post - mirror a cast to X and Bluesky in the same action.
  • Everything previews before sending. The confirm step is the point - this app never posts on its own.

Grow - your numbers

  • Followers, following, Neynar score, and a day-by-day follower sparkline that builds up as you use the app.
  • Your top fans - who engages you most. Reply back; it compounds.
  • People to engage next - suggestions worth a first touch.
  • Your top casts - ranked by engagement so you learn what lands.
  • Storage - Farcaster protocol storage units, with a warning when casts are at capacity and pruning.
  • Zora coin - if you have a Creator Coin, its market cap and 24h move show here, linked to the coin page.

Empire - the tokenless-first playbook

Empire Builder (empirebuilder.world) wraps a project or community in leaderboards, boosters, and a treasury - with or without a token. The playbook this tab is built around: don't launch a token first. Stand up a tokenless empire, build real energy on a leaderboard, and tokenize later, when there's something for the token to amplify. The token adds to what you built; it never has to be the foundation.

What's on the tab:

  • Your empire card - leaderboard count, boosters, recent distributions/burns/airdrops, and your own rank if you're on your top leaderboard.
  • view leaderboard - the top 25, ranked, with Farcaster names.
  • Look up a distribution - paste any distribution transaction hash and see exactly who got paid and how much. On-chain receipts, human-readable.
  • Top empires - the ecosystem-wide list, read-only.
  • Capabilities catalog - every Empire Builder API surface this app knows about, marked live vs documented. It doubles as the build roadmap.

Create a tokenless empire (step by step)

What you need: a wallet (MetaMask, Coinbase Wallet, or open the app inside a Farcaster client and use its built-in wallet) and about a minute. No token. No funds move.
  1. Empire tab, tap + create a tokenless empire.
  2. Name it. Bio and logo are optional and editable later.
  3. Connect wallet. The wallet you connect becomes the empire's owner (its "guardian") - use the one you mean to keep.
  4. Tap sign & deploy. Your wallet shows the exact message you're signing - it's a plain sentence naming your empire, nothing hidden. Signing is the approval; the app never holds a key and can't deploy anything without you.
  5. Done - you get the empire's id and treasury address. Put the owner wallet into config.js (empireOwnerWallet) and the tab shows your empire card from then on.
Why there's no "attach to my Farcaster profile" option: Empire Builder supports it, but a profile-attached empire is permanent - that profile can never create another one. Deliberately left out of this app; create it standalone instead.

Add a booster (step by step)

A booster multiplies leaderboard points for people who hold something - a token, an NFT, or a reputation score. It's how you reward your actual community: hold the thing, your engagement counts more.

  1. Empire tab, tap + add booster on your empire card.
  2. Pick the type: ERC20 (hold a token), NFT (hold from a collection), or reputation (QUOTIENT score).
  3. Paste the token/NFT contract address (skipped for reputation).
  4. Set the multiplier - 1.1 to 5.0. Holding qualifies you; the multiplier is how hard it counts.
  5. Set the minimum to qualify in normal token amounts - e.g. 1000000 for a 1M-token floor. Leave decimals at 18 unless the token is unusual; the app does the raw-unit math exactly.
  6. Name + symbol are just for display on Empire Builder. Connect wallet, sign, done. It shows on the tab within a minute.

POIDH - bounties

POIDH ("pics or it didn't happen", poidh.xyz) is permissionless bounties on Base: someone funds a pot, people submit proof, the pot pays out. The POIDH tab reads it live:

  • Round chips - the BCZ-issued bounty rounds (R1 onward), one tap each.
  • Any bounty by id - paste a number, get the pot, status, description, and every claim with its image.
  • Claiming happens on poidh.xyz itself (it's an on-chain action from your wallet). This tab is the reader; the claim button lives with the money.
